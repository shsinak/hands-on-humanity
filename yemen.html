Yemen:

A humanitarian crisis in the heart of the Arabian Peninsula

Photo by ALMWALD ALEZZEY on Unsplash

General Information

Yemen, located at the southern end of the Arabian Peninsula, boasts a rich history that spans over three millennia. Its strategic position made it a hub for ancient civilizations such as the Sabaeans, Minaeans, and Himyarites, who thrived on the lucrative spice trade. The region is often associated with the legendary Queen of Sheba and was referred to by the Romans as "Arabia Felix," meaning "Fertile Arabia" or "Happy Arabia," due to its prosperity.

In the 7th century, Yemen embraced Islam, integrating into the broader Islamic world. The modern Republic of Yemen was established in May 1990, unifying the Yemen Arab Republic (North Yemen) and the People's Democratic Republic of Yemen (South Yemen).

Since 2015, Yemen has been engulfed in a devastating conflict that has precipitated one of the world's worst humanitarian crises. As of recent estimates, approximately 21.6 million people—two-thirds of the population—are in dire need of humanitarian assistance. The conflict has displaced over 4.5 million individuals, many multiple times, exacerbating their vulnerability. The war has also led to severe food insecurity, with more than 17 million people experiencing acute hunger.

The situation has been further complicated by regional dynamics. Israeli military actions have extended into Yemen, targeting areas associated with Houthi rebels. For instance, Israeli airstrikes have hit multiple locations in Yemen, leading to Houthi retaliations with missiles and drones aimed at Israel, which were intercepted. These developments have heightened regional tensions and added complexity to Yemen's internal conflict.

Important Terms

Humanitarian Crisis
A situation where humanitarian needs are so large and complex that they require significant external assistance and a multi-sectoral response, involving a wide range of international humanitarian actors
Food Insecurity
A situation that exists when all people, at all times, have physical, social and economic access to sufficient, safe and nutritious food that meets their dietary needs and food preferences for an active and healthy life.
Displacement
The forced or involuntary movement of people from their homes or places of habitual residence, often due to conflict, violence, human rights violations, or natural or human-made disasters.
